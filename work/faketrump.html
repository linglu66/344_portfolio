We trained a state-of-the-art model (GPT-2) to generate crazy realistic-sounding tweets from Donald Trump. We made a classifier to differentiate real from fake, and then developed a simple game to see how well humans can do vs machines.

Check out the game here, and our code here.

The Story

This project started from a crazy "what if we did this" and became a real game that garnered thousands of visits and discussion from around the world. GPT-2 had released a tune-able model and my coworker Helen came up with the idea of generating Trump tweets, and everything just went from there.

Data and Model

We scraped Donald Trump's Twitter feed and fine-tuned GPT2 to generate 10,000 fake tweets. The classifier I trained (LSTM) could distinguish between fake and real tweets to 70% accuracy.

The Game

Inspired by arxiv vs snarxiv, I wanted to develop a similar game where users would try to find the ML-generated fake from pairs of real and fake tweets. The application would randomly choose (without replacement) 10 real and fake tweets from our set of 10,000.

Results

Our game made the rounds through the machine learning community on Twitter and Reddit, and had over 17000 hits in 16 countries.
