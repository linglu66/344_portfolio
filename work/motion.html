Teaching myself how to make things move

A fun challenge, inspired by Tian Xu's 100 Days of motion design

  • Role

    Swiss Army Knife

  • Tools

    Sketch, Flow

  • Time

    Ongoing

Motion is like a whole other dimension to design in, which is pretty darn cool. Where I was previously making flat, static prototypes - now I can see them blink, slide, and come to life.

One of the biggest things I struggle with is getting distracted by new things to try. Between 3d rendering, making games in Unity, simulating machine learning algorithms, and learning javascript, sometimes I feel like my brain is going in five different directions. This challenge is a way for me to dive into a topic I've been eyeing for a while, commit to it and expand my designers toolkit.

I've just started this challenge and will be updating daily with my work! I'll also do a brief summary every few chunk of days or so.

Day 1

Squares based off of one of Flow's default timelines.

Good bits:

  • My PM said he liked the colours. It's not normally a palette I would go for, but I think it looks kinda cool, with that funky, neon futuristic vibe.
  • How the square overshoots a bit when its rising up - I read that it makes the elements look more natural, like how objects in real life compress and reshape.
Things to work on:
  • I realize now that it's too fast.
  • The motion isn't as smooth as I'd like it to be, maybe tinker with the timing curve + more overshooting.
  • More relative depth - the two elements 'on the ground' shouldn't actually be on the same plane, since one is slightly behind. Could add a shadow to the one sliding behind.
  • Might be cool to re-do this isometrically.
