Sept 2020 - School Team Project
Patient Monitoring for Home Caretakers

As part of our group work for a design course at the Univeristy of Toronto, I collaborated with a team of three engineering students to design and prototype a mobile application to help home caretakers better monitor and record their patient’s health metrics. We worked on this project over the course of a semester, from research and problem statement to final high fidelity prototype.

Understanding

Understanding the Problem

From a list of topics, our team decided to explore “preventative patient monitoring” in further detail. We conducted secondary research through articles and papers like this and this, and discovered an underserved group of users in the market: home caretakers.

Research Findings

Exploration

User Interviews

Myself and another teammate, Michael, who were both familiar with user interviews split off to speak to three home caretakers and patients to learn more about their needs and define requirements. Our other teammates focused more on secondary research. We created a research field guide, and held open-ended interviews to let users speak freely about their experiences and discover pain points.

Quotes from Interviews
Definitions

Requirements

From the interviews and initial research, we understood the problem space better and defined a clear set of requirements for our solution going forward.

Our research pinpointed primary needs of our users, like needing an easy way to keep track of patient health metrics, as well as being able to easily communicate with their doctors.

Iteration

Usability Testing

Then in pairs of two, we combined our prototypes to refine and explore a broader feature set.

We initially expected this process to be lengthy to settle on a mix of two different designs, but realized that doing quick iterations, assessing the result and redoing the prototype was the best approach to getting it done.

We then assessed both prototypes in virtual usability testing sessions with our classmates, and the results are below.

Comparison of System Usability Scores for both prototypes
High Fidelity

Final Product

After rounds of usability testing and internal evaluation, we worked together live on Figma, to create our high-fidelity prototype. Being the most experienced team member with design, I took the lead in driving design decisions and visual direction.

Reflection

Working completely remotely for this project was definitely a challenge. Finding times where our entire team was available, getting to know each other solely through calls, and dealing with internet issues made the experience less than ideal

However to counter these issues and barriers of working remotely, we created a team charter to identify our team values, expectations, and goals. I believe having an explicit conversation about all those things and agreeing to be open and honest about our opinions helped us successfully finish our project in these settings.

I also learned a lot about challenging each others ideas and bringing up opposing opinions - especially in calls where we don’t see each other’s faces or body language, the words we use and tone we say them in matter a lot.
