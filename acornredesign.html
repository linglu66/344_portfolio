ACORN Redesign

Punchlines are important for grabbing attention.

Overview

After two years of getting to know ACORN (The Accessible Campus Online Resource Network), I noticed some inefficiencies in the design, and wanted to try my hand a re-making the interface. Initially I leaned towards a complete re-design, however after a thorough usability analysis on the current design, decided that the content frame was there and that the interface just needed a clean-up.

Goals

  • Identify the strengths and weaknesses of the current interface
  • Re-design it to capitalize on the strengths and reduce clutter and confusion

Concept Generation & Wireframes

The initial brainstorm and some possible design directions.

Design Analysis

A sample of the analysis done - identifying parts that could be confusing or elements that need reworking.

Results

I wanted to stick to the "if it's not broken, don't fix it" concept for this redesign, since functionality-wise there isn't anything terribly wrong with ACORN. Just some visual aspects that came across as awkward or clunky that I wanted to try to improve in my version.
